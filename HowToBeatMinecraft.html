Acquiring Items

In this section of the guide, you will learn how to acquire the items necessary to survive beyond the overworld.

Preparing for Caves

When you first create a survival world, before anything else, you should gather wood from trees of any type.
Once you gather about 5 logs, turn them into planks by pressing E to open your inventory and placing them in the
crafting area. Once you've done that, create a crafting table by filling the previously mentioned crafting area
with planks. After this is done, follow the recipes shown in the images below.

Immediately after obtaining the pickaxe, break the blocks beneath you (in most biomes) to go underground
and acquire cobblestone, which you can use to create tools using the recipes shown in the video below.

At this point, your priority should be getting food. The most common mobs that drop food are pigs, cows, chickens, and sheep.
There are other sources of food, but for now, prioritize these.

Exploring Caves

Now, it's time to find a cave. Caves are where ores are acquired, which you can use to create stronger armor and tools.
It's best to understand how to craft armor without consulting the recipe book, which can be found to the left of the
crafting HUD. Armor recipes can be seen in the video below.

At this point, it's been a while since you started your world. You're probably a bit hungry, but you've got food. The only
issue is that it's raw, right? Worry not, for the most common resources that caves yield are cobblestone and coal, which are
the two materials it takes to craft and operate a furnace. Using your stone pickaxe, acquire about a stack of coal (stack = 64)
and arrange 8 cobblestone blocks along the outer ends of the crafting HUD to create a furnace. As shown in the image below
to smelt items in a furnace, place your fuel in the bottom space, and that which you wish to smelt in the top space. Using
the furnace, cook your food. Later, you will smelt iron and gold using the furnace.

Now that you've got cooked food and you know how to use a furnace, it's time to descend. Some caves stop at dead ends, so
it's up to you to find one that goes deep enough to find the ores you're looking for. Diamonds, the most valuable ore that can
be found in the overworld, are most common at Y-coordinate 12 (You can enable coordinates in your settings). As you descend the
cave, you'll encounter a multitude of dangerous creatures. There are creepers, which explode when standing close to you for a
period of time, there are skeletons, which shoot you with bows and arrows, and there are zombies, which slowly walk towards you
to attack you.


After you've got your coal, mine iron ore, smelt it, and make full iron armor before anything else. This will guarantee you to
survive the deadly creeper explosion, which can instantly kill you if you've got nothing to protect you and easily end your
world early on. Make iron tools using the recipes shown previously, but using iron ingots in place of cobblestone. Descend towards
Y-coordinate 12, and use the ore rarity table shown below.

Preparing for the Nether

After spending some time in the lower regions of a cave, you'll likely have acquired yourself some valuable materials.
After you've finished gathering ores in a cave, the next step is to go to the nether. To do this, you will need to build
a nether portal, as can be seen in the image below.

A Nether Portal is simply comprised of 10 obsidian blocks placed in a specific shape. As you can see in the image,
the player is holding a flint and steel, which can be crafted by placing an iron ingot and a filnt, which can be acquired
by breaking gravel, in any particular order in the crafting HUD. This is because the portal must be lit with a fire charge
in order to activate. There are two ways to build a nether portal. At this point in the game, obsidian can only be mined with
a diamond pickaxe. Using one, you can mine 10 obsidian, build the shape of the portal shown in the image above, and light it
with the flint and steel. The other way to build the portal is to use water and lava buckets. When flowing water touches a lava
source block, it creates obsidian. Taking advantage of this, you can create a nether portal, as is demonstrated in the video below.

Locating the Fortress

You may be wondering why you must go to the Nether in order to complete the game. The End dimension is, as the name would
suggest, the last dimension the player must complete in order to beat Minecraft. More about the End dimension will be
explained later on in the guide, but for now, all you need to know is that there is an item that you must obtain within
the Nether in order to get to the End, and that item is the blaze rod. Blaze rods are dropped by the blaze mob, which can
be found in Nether Fortresses Blazes are shown in the image below.

Many biomes are found in the Nether, as of update 1.16. These include the Nether Wastes, the Soul Sand Valley, the Crimson Forest,
the Warped Forest, and the Basalt Deltas. Each biome hosts a different variety of mobs, but for now, because we only care about
completing the game, we'll just focus on finding the Fortress, which can be found in all biomes. There's no trick to finding it,
you've just got to search around the Nether until you happen to come accross it. The Nether Fortress can be seen in the image below.

Inside the Fortress

The Nether Fortress is a dangerous place to be. It's the only place in the entirety of the game where both blazes and
wither skeletons spawn, which are two of the most dangerous mobs a player can encounter. Both apply damage-over-time
effects in the form of withering and burning, which can make quick work of a player when coupled with the fact that these
mobs tend to attack in groups, meaning that you'll almost always be afflicted with one of these harmful ailments. The best
way to deal with these mobs is to use a shield, which you can place in your offhand by hovering over it in your inventory
and pressing the F key. The crafting recipe for shields can be seen below. Because the Nether Fortress comprises of narrow
hallways, more often than not, if you're being overwhelmed by dangerous mobs, you can block them off.

Now that you know how to deal with these dangerous mobs, let's talk loot, because there's some great loot to be found
in these fortresses. The most valuable items that can be found in the Nether Fortress, besides blazes rods, are diamond,
saddles, and horse armor. Saddles do not have a crafting recipe as of 1.16, so the only other two means of obtaining
them are through dungeons and ravagers during village raids. The two most worthwhile uses for saddles are horses and
striders, but in this guide, we won't be delving too far into that. Nether fortresses are also the only places in the
game in which nether warts can be found.


Back to the main idea: as mentioned before, the Nether Fortress is where you can acquire blaze rods. Once again,
blaze rods are dropped by the blaze mob, which can be found scattered about the fortress, but the easiest way to get
all of the blaze rods is to find the blaze spawner and kill them as they spawn. Blaze rods have about a 50% chance of
dropping from blazes on kill, and you want about 12 rods. In the next section of the guide, the reason you needed
blaze rods in the first place will be explained.

Preparing for the End

This is the final stretch, but there is a lot of preparing to do before you can go to the End and fight the dragon.
Before anything else, you need to have the Eye of Ender in order to be able to locate the Stronghold,
the naturally occuring structure in the overworld, found deep underground, in which the End Portal lies.
The End dimension, as you can see in the image below, consists of one large island outlined by 10 obsidian pillars, with
an End Crystal sitting at the top of each. These crystals heal the dragon with such efficiency that it
isn't even worth attacking the dragon until each one is destroyed. Luckily, they're fairly easy to break, so long as you
come to the fight with a bow and plenty of arrows. Some of the crystals are protected by Iron Bars, but
the player can shoot arrows from beneath said bars in order to hit the crystals.

As you would probably expect, the End dimension is home to Endermen. Endermen are neutral mobs that become hostile
upon either being hit, or being looked at directly in the eyes. While fighting the Ender Dragon, it can be hard not to
accidentally aggravate endermen, even though you'll be looking upwards for the most part. One item you could consider
bringing is the Carved Pumpkin, which allows you to look at endermen without aggravating them. This isn't ideal,
however, because wearing a carved pumpkin both obscures your vision and occupies your helmet slot. The most important
item to bring is a Water Bucket. Essentially, you'll use it to break your fall when the dragon uses
its wing attack to throw you into the air. This requires timing and practice, so keep that in mind before going to the End.

The Stronghold

Before you can locate the Stronghold, you need to acquire
12 enderpearls at the bare minimum. You've already been through the Nether, so you have more than enough blaze rods.
Take the blaze rods, put them in the crafting hud with nothing else, and you'll create blaze powder. Combine
blaze powder and ender pearls in no particular order, and you'll create Eyes of Ender.

Once you have at least 12 eyes of Ender and you're fully prepared to take on the dragon, throw an eye by hovering
over it in your inventory and right clicking. The eye will float in the direction of the Stronghold, and from there,
you just follow it. Each time you throw an Eye of Ender, there is a 1/5 chance of it breaking, which is why you may
have wanted more than 12 eyes. If you throw the eye while you're above the stronghold, it will go straight into the
ground, which is how you'll know you've found it. At that point, dig straight down until you're in the Stronghold
(shown below).

Once you're in the Stronghold, your goal is to find the portal room (shown below). The portal consists of 12 slots
that must be filled with Eyes of Ender in order to activate. Each slot has a 10% chance of already being filled.
Like the Nether Fortress, Strongholds contain valuable loot. Often, in the chests that lie scattered about the
stronghold, you'll find ender pearls, which you can use to fill the portal. One of the most valuable rooms you'll
find is the library, where two chests are always to be found. These chests contain enchantment books which often
have enchantments that'll make fighting the dragon significantly easier.

Fighting the Dragon

The duration of the dragon fight varies depending on luck. Sometimes, it likes to be fly around far away in
the sky while you wait helplessly for it to return to the center of the large island. Other times, it stays in
the center doing absolutely nothing while you bash it relentlessly with an axe, or blow it up with beds.
Beds blow up in dimensions that aren't the overworld, which is something that you can take advantage of
if your intention is to kill the dragon as quickly as possible. Regardless of what you intend to do, your
first course of action will always be to destroy each of the 10 crystals that heal the dragon using a bow
and arrow. As you're doing this, the dragon will shoot fireballs at you that leave a lingering pool of
damage in the spot that they land on. As mentioned before, the dragon will use its wings to fling you
into the sky, but you can use a water bucket to break your fall if you've got the timing down. Endermen
cannot touch water, so if you're being overwhelmed by them, you can also use your waterbucket to create
a sort of barrier that they have no way to breach.
